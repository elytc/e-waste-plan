## 1. The Problem We're Solving

Electronic waste (e-waste) is the fastest-growing type of waste in the world.
Old computers, phones, TVs, printers, and other electronics contain
both valuable materials (copper, gold, aluminum) and hazardous ones
(lead, mercury, cadmium) that shouldn't end up in landfills.

**The rural challenge:** In a remote area like Ely, people often have
nowhere convenient to drop off old electronics. The nearest recycling
centers may be hours away. That means devices pile up in garages,
basements, and sheds - or worse, end up in the trash.

### Background

> Minnesota generates roughly 266 million pounds of e-waste per year,
> but only about 20-25% gets properly recycled.
> Source: [RECA](https://reca-us.org/)

> Right now, we are dumping or exporting the majority of valuable metals from our electronic waste.
> If we recycle all of our e-waste in the State of Minnesota, we could create over 1,700 jobs.
> Source: [RECA](https://reca-us.org/)

### 4.1 Step-by-Step

1. **Community Outreach** - Let people know when and where to bring devices
2. **Collection** - Accept devices at events or our drop-off location
3. **Intake & Logging** - Record what we receive (type, condition, donor)
4. **Sorting** - Separate by type and condition
5. **Data Wiping** - Erase any personal data from storage devices
6. **Testing** - Check if anything is still usable (potential resale/reuse)
7. **Staging** - Organize and palletize for transport
8. **Transport** - Deliver to our certified recycling partner
9. **Documentation** - Track what was recycled for reporting

## 8. Legal and Safety Stuff

This isn't the most exciting part, but it's important.

### Minnesota Regulations

- **Minnesota Electronics Recycling Act** - Governs how e-waste must be handled
- **MPCA Registration** - Collectors that operate ongoing programs must register
  by July 15 each year
- **Certified Recyclers** - We must send our collected items to an MPCA-registered
  recycler (look for R2 or e-Stewards certification)
- **Hazardous Materials** - Lead, mercury, and cadmium in electronics are regulated;
  we need to handle them properly

### Safety Rules for Our Team

- [ ] Always wear gloves when handling old electronics
- [ ] Never open CRT monitors or TVs (they can hold dangerous electrical charge)
- [ ] Store lithium batteries separately in a fireproof container
- [ ] Wash hands after handling electronics
- [ ] Lift with your legs - old TVs and printers are heavy!
- [ ] No food or drinks in the sorting area
